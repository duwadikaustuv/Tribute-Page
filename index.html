Nelson Mandela

The man fought against apartheid and promoted racial equality.

Time line of Nelson Mandela's life:

Early Life and Education

  • July 18, 1918: Nelson Rolihlahla Mandela is born in the village of Mvezo in Umtata, then part of South Africa's Cape Province.
  • 1930: Mandela's father dies, and he is sent to live with the regent of the Tembu people, Chief Jongintaba Dalindyebo.
  • 1939: Mandela enrolls at the University of Fort Hare, a premier institution for black Africans in South Africa.
  • 1940: Mandela is expelled from Fort Hare for participating in a student protest.

Early Activism

  • 1941: Mandela moves to Johannesburg, where he works in various jobs and completes his BA degree via correspondence through the University of South Africa.
  • 1943: Mandela begins studying law at the University of Witwatersrand.
  • 1944: Mandela joins the African National Congress (ANC) and helps form the ANC Youth League.

Political Struggle

  • 1948: The National Party comes to power in South Africa and begins implementing apartheid policies.
  • 1952: Mandela and Oliver Tambo open the first black law firm in South Africa. Mandela plays a key role in the Defiance Campaign against apartheid laws.
  • 1956: Mandela and 155 others are arrested and charged with treason in the Treason Trial, which lasts until 1961, ending in acquittals for all.

Imprisonment

  • 1960: The Sharpeville Massacre occurs, leading to the banning of the ANC. Mandela goes underground.
  • 1961: Mandela co-founds Umkhonto we Sizwe (Spear of the Nation), the armed wing of the ANC.
  • August 5, 1962: Mandela is arrested and later sentenced to five years in prison for incitement and leaving the country without a passport.
  • 1963: While serving his sentence, Mandela is brought to trial again in the Rivonia Trial.
  • June 12, 1964: Mandela and seven others are sentenced to life imprisonment. Mandela is sent to Robben Island Prison, where he spends 18 of his 27 years in prison.

Release and Presidency

  • February 11, 1990: Mandela is released from prison after 27 years.
  • 1991: Mandela is elected President of the ANC.
  • 1993: Mandela and South African President F.W. de Klerk are awarded the Nobel Peace Prize.
  • April 27, 1994: South Africa holds its first multiracial elections. The ANC wins, and Mandela becomes South Africa's first black president.
  • May 10, 1994:Mandela is inaugurated as President of South Africa.

Later Life and Legacy

  • 1999: Mandela steps down as president, succeeded by Thabo Mbeki.
  • 2004: Mandela announces his retirement from public life.
  • 2007: Mandela forms The Elders, a group of global leaders focused on peace and human rights.
  • December 5, 2013: Nelson Mandela dies at the age of 95.

Posthumous Recognition

  • December 15, 2013: Mandela is buried in Qunu, his ancestral village.
  • July 18, 2018:
  • The centenary of Mandela's birth is marked by global celebrations and reflections on his legacy.
